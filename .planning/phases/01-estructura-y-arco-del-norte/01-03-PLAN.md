Content requirements:
- **Certeza indicator:** icon `⚖`, label `Interpretacion historiografica`
- **Image:** Search Wikimedia Commons for a portrait of Jose de San Martin. San Martin portraits are widely available. Try `File:Jose_de_San_Martin.jpg` or the well-known portrait by Jean-Baptiste Madou. Verify URL resolves.
- **Year:** `1814 – 1817`
- **Title:** `¿Por que Chile? — la leccion del norte que cambio la estrategia`
- **Excerpt (2-3 sentences):** Cover: three expediciones al Alto Peru habian fracasado en cinco anos. San Martin, gobernador de Cuyo desde 1814, concluyo que la ruta del norte era inviable y propuso cruzar los Andes por Chile para atacar Lima por mar. Esta interpretacion — las derrotas del norte forzaron la alternativa andina — es una de las tesis centrales de la historiografia de la independencia.
- **Card-detail:**
  First, the blockquote (San Martin's 1814 letter requesting the governorship of Cuyo, which reveals his strategic thinking):
  ```html
  <blockquote class="card-opinion__quote">
    <p class="card-opinion__text">«La patria no hara camino por este lado del norte. [...] Ya le he dicho a usted mi secreto: un pequeno ejercito bien disciplinado en Mendoza para pasar a Chile y acabar alli con los godos [...] aliando las fuerzas, pasaremos por mar a tomar Lima.»</p>
    <footer class="card-opinion__attribution">
      <span class="card-opinion__author">Jose de San Martin</span>
      <span class="card-opinion__context">Carta a Nicolas Rodriguez Pena, 22 de abril de 1814. En Museo Historico Nacional, Documentos del Archivo de San Martin, t. II.</span>
    </footer>
  </blockquote>
  ```

  Then 2-3 interpretive paragraphs:
  (1) The documented pattern — Suipacha (1810) was the only victory; Huaqui (1811), Vilcapugio/Ayohuma (1813), and Sipe-Sipe (1815) were catastrophic defeats. The Alto Peru presented structural military disadvantages: extended supply lines from Tucuman, hostile terrain above 3000m, royalist forces reinforced from Lima, and local elites increasingly hostile to Buenos Aires;
  (2) San Martin's alternative — from Cuyo, San Martin organized the Ejercito de los Andes and crossed to Chile in January 1817, a campaign that succeeded where three northern expeditions had failed. Mitre's thesis (1887) — that San Martin conceived the plan precisely because the northern route had been proven impossible — remains the dominant interpretation;
  (3) Historiographic nuance — frame in the voice of historians: "Mitre senala que...", "Halperin Donghi observa que...". Note that some historians (Bragoni, 2019) argue San Martin's plan was also influenced by Chile's own internal situation and the alliance with O'Higgins, not solely by northern failures. The card should present both views but foreground the northern-failure thesis as the established mainstream interpretation.

- **Footer cite:** `Mitre, B., Historia de San Martin, Buenos Aires, 1887. Halperin Donghi, T., Revolucion y guerra, Buenos Aires, Siglo XXI, 1972. Bragoni, B., San Martin: una biografia politica del libertador, Buenos Aires, Edhasa, 2019.`

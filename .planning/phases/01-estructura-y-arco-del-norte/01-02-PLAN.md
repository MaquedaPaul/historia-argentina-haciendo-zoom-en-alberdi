**Card: ALTO-03 — Exodo Jujeno (23 de agosto de 1812)**

Structure: `article.event-card.card-hecho.reveal.reveal-slide` with `data-certeza="hecho"` and `style="--reveal-delay: 160ms"`.

Content requirements:
- **Certeza indicator:** icon `✓`, label `Hecho documentado`
- **Image:** Use a portrait of Manuel Belgrano from Wikimedia Commons. Belgrano portraits are widely available. Search for `File:Manuel_Belgrano.jpg` or similar. Verify URL resolves before including. The UI-SPEC confirms "Portrait of Belgrano (widely available)" as fallback.
- **Year:** `23 de agosto de 1812`
- **Title:** `El Exodo Jujeno — tierra arrasada para salvar la revolucion`
- **Excerpt (2-3 sentences):** Cover: Belgrano, newly appointed commander of the Ejercito del Norte after Huaqui, ordered the complete evacuation of San Salvador de Jujuy — civilian population, livestock, crops, all resources. Everything that could not be moved was burned to deny supplies to the advancing royalist army under Pio Tristan.
- **Card-detail (2-3 paragraphs):**
  (1) Context — after Huaqui, the Junta ordered Belgrano to retreat to Cordoba without engaging the enemy; Belgrano judged that abandoning Jujuy and Salta without resistance would demoralize the population irreversibly; he decided on a scorched-earth retreat instead;
  (2) The exodus — on 29 July 1812 Belgrano issued the bando ordering evacuation; on 23 August the column departed; the entire population of Jujuy — estimated at several thousand people — marched south toward Tucuman carrying what they could, while rearguard forces destroyed crops, buildings, and supplies; the royalist army entered an empty, devastated city;
  (3) Significance — the exodus bought time and denied Tristan's army resupply, contributing to the patriot victories at Tucuman (24 Sept 1812) and Salta (20 Feb 1813); it remains one of the most dramatic episodes of the Argentine independence wars and is commemorated annually in Jujuy.

- **Footer cite:** `Mitre, B., Historia de Belgrano, Buenos Aires, 1887. Paz, J. M., Memorias postumas, Buenos Aires, 1855. Bidondo, E., Historia de Jujuy, Buenos Aires, Plus Ultra, 1980.`

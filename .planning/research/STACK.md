## Academic Historians — Primary Sources

### Tier 1: Academically Rigorous, Essential

| Historian | Work | Year | Publisher | Why Use |
|-----------|------|------|-----------|---------|
| Tulio Halperin Donghi | *Revolucion y guerra: Formacion de una elite dirigente en la Argentina criolla* | 1972 (updated 2014) | Siglo XXI | Standard reference for structural analysis of the 1810 revolution and the disarticulation of Alto Peru commercial circuits. Foundational for the "why the north was lost" narrative arc. |
| Sara Mata | *Los gauchos de Guemes: Guerras de independencia y conflicto social* | 2008 | Sudamericana | Only monograph-length academic treatment of the guerra gaucha from a social history perspective. Covers Salta society, counter-revolution, rural insurrection, Guemes as governor (1815-1821). CONICET researcher at Universidad Nacional de Salta. |
| Bartolome Mitre | *Historia de Belgrano y de la independencia argentina* | 1858-1859 (multiple editions) | Various | Foundational primary historiography. Covers all Alto Peru campaigns in detail, contains maps of Huaqui and other battles. Digitized on Internet Archive. Use as historical source, not as uncontested truth — Mitre had a portenio bias critiqued by Alberdi himself. |
| Gabriel Di Meglio | Interviews and academic articles on guerra gaucha | 2021 (bicentenary) | UBA / UNSAM | Contemporary academic consensus voice. Argues the guerra gaucha was structurally necessary for San Martin's strategy. States clearly: "Sin la Guerra Gaucha, la Independencia no podria haber sido ganada." Useful for framing the narrative arc. |

### Tier 2: Useful Supporting Sources

| Historian | Work | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Gustavo Paz | Articles on Jujuy and the Guemes system | Complements Sara Mata; focuses on Jujuy side of northern frontier |
| Ricardo Levene | *Historia de la Nacion Argentina* (dir.) | Multi-volume academy-directed work; republished Mitre. Use for factual chronology, not interpretation. |
| Jorge Gelman | Articles on rural economy and independence | Contextualizes gaucho social class; available via CONICET repositories |

## What NOT to Use

| Source | Why Avoid | Use Instead |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| elhistoriador.com.ar (Felipe Pigna) | Academic consensus (Halperin Donghi, Luis Alberto Romero) criticizes Pigna for anachronism, misquoting, and lack of historiographical rigor. Written for mass audiences, not scholarly use. | Sara Mata, Di Meglio, Halperin Donghi for argumentation. Pigna's factual timelines can be spot-checked against academic sources. |
| Revisionismo historico websites (lagazeta.com.ar, etc.) | Revisionist current that romanticizes caudillos and gauchos for political purposes; treats folklore as history; originated in 1930s nationalist politics. Presents "two Argentinas" framework as essential truth rather than historical construction. | Peer-reviewed sources via CONICET (redalyc.org, scielo.org.ar) |
| Fandom/Military Wiki pages | Compiled from Wikipedia, no original research, errors propagate. | Wikipedia as starting point only; verify every fact against academic sources. |
| todo-argentina.net, elarcondelahistoria.com | Popular aggregator sites with no citations or methodology. | Internet Archive primary documents |
| Modern paintings commissioned for commemorative events | Risk of anachronistic detail (uniforms, faces, etc.) presented as historical | Contemporary period portraits and maps only, with clear date attribution |

## Alternatives Considered

| Recommended | Alternative | When to Use Alternative |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------------|
| Sara Mata (social history) | Bernardo Frias *Historia del general Guemes* (1902-1911) | Frias has useful primary detail but is hagiographic and pre-academic; use only for specific quotes or data points with caveat |
| Halperin Donghi for structural analysis | John Lynch *The Spanish American Revolutions 1808-1826* (1973, Norton) | Lynch is excellent for comparative Latin American context but less Argentina-specific; use if writing connects to broader independence movements |
| Wikimedia Commons verified PD images | BNE (Biblioteca Nacional Espana) digital collections | BNE has Spanish military portraits (Pezuela, Goyeneche) if Commons lacks them; license is more complex |

## Stack Patterns by Content Card

**For Alto Peru battle cards (Huaqui, Salta, Vilcapugio, Ayohuma, Sipe-Sipe):**
- Image source: Wikimedia `Category:Battles_of_the_Argentine_War_of_Independence` (direct files) + `Category:Manuel_Belgrano`
- Text source: Mitre *Historia de Belgrano* for factual chronology + Halperin Donghi for structural causality
- Certainty level: Most tactical facts = HECHO; causation claims = OPINION (historiographical debate exists)

**For Guemes and guerra gaucha cards:**
- Image source: `Category:Martin_Miguel_de_Guemes` (22 files) + `Category:Gauchos_of_Argentina` (133 files)
- Text source: Sara Mata 2008 as primary academic reference + Di Meglio for modern synthesis
- Certainty level: Guerrilla tactics and outcomes = HECHO; interpretation of social character (class struggle vs. patriotism) = OPINION

**For republiquetas cards (Azurduy, Warnes, Padilla):**
- Image source: `Category:Juana_Azurduy` (14 files) — no Commons categories found for Warnes or Padilla
- Text source: Wikipedia *Republiquetas* article as entry point, verified against academic sources. Sara Mata also covers Azurduy in the Guemes context.
- Certainty level: Dates and locations = HECHO; leadership attribution = some historiographical debate = flag as OPINION where contested

**For narrative arc card ("por que el norte se perdio"):**
- Image source: None specific — use Belgrano portrait + San Martin portrait combination
- Text source: Halperin Donghi structural argument + Di Meglio quotes
- This is the interpretive card: certeza level = OPINION explicitly

**Key research findings:** All facts below are verified across multiple secondary sources consistent with the primary Weinberg 1977 compilation.

1. **Echeverría's return (1830):** Left Buenos Aires October 1825 as "comerciante", returned late June 1830 as "literato". Spent four years at the Sorbonne, Athénée, and with private tutors. Steeped in Saint-Simon, Leroux, Byron, Hugo, Goethe. Registered at the Aduana on 28 June 1830. Found Rosas in government with *facultades extraordinarias* recently renewed — complete political reversal from Rivadavia's era.

2. **1832–1834 — informal gatherings:** From 1832, a group of young intellectuals began meeting informally at Marcos Sastre's librería. Key members at this early stage: Alberdi, Gutiérrez. Echeverría was present separately, gaining literary reputation through *Los Consuelos* (November 1834) — his first collection of romantic poems, which marked him immediately as a literary leader. Sources confirm the informal circle around the librería predates the formal Salón.

3. **1834–1835 — Echeverría meets Gutiérrez, then Alberdi joins:** The academic source (Academia Nacional de Derecho, CONICET PDF) is precise: "En 1834, Esteban Echeverría y Juan María Gutiérrez tenían ya un estrecho contacto y disfrutaban una fraternal amistad. Tenían por costumbre realizar largas caminatas por los extramuros de la ciudad." Alberdi joined these walks "ya en 1835". This is the earliest documented personal encounter sequence: Echeverría–Gutiérrez first (1834), Alberdi joins in 1835. This is a **card-hecho** — well-documented in secondary literature citing Alberdi's own memoirs.

4. **Alberdi's description of the walks:** Alberdi himself wrote that the walks and conversations "fueron un constante estudio libre, sin plan ni sistema, mezclado a menudo a diversiones y pasatiempos" — cited in the same academic PDF. Usable as an attributed quote (Alberdi's memoirs).

5. **Formal Salón founding date — DISCREPANCY requiring card-nota-certeza:**
   - Weinberg (1977) and Biblioteca del Congreso: **26 June 1837**
   - Multiple secondary sources: **23 June 1837**
   - Actosenlaescuela.com: "Se cree que inició actividades el 23 de junio de 1837"
   - The primary pamphlet published in 1837 (*Discursos pronunciados el día de la apertura del Salón Literario*) exists at SAS-Space (London) — it gives no date in the metadata available
   - Resolution: the site already uses "26 de junio de 1837" (consistent with Weinberg, the compiled primary source). Use 26 June as the established date with a card-nota-certeza noting the 23 June alternative.

6. **The inaugural act:** Three discourses, well-documented:
   - Marcos Sastre: *Ojeada filosófica sobre el estado presente y la suerte futura de la nación argentina* — hosted, opened
   - Juan Bautista Alberdi: *Doble armonía entre el objeto de esta institución con una exigencia de nuestro desarrollo social…* — philosophical keynote
   - Juan María Gutiérrez: *Fisonomía del saber español: cuál deba ser entre nosotros* — cultural rupture argument
   - The act was **presided over by Vicente López y Planes** (author of the Himno Nacional) — confirmed by historiaybiografias.com and elarcondelahistoria.com
   - **Echeverría was absent** from the inaugural but sent two cantos of *La Cautiva* read in the session — confirmed by multiple sources (elarcondelahistoria.com, librerialatijera.com, actosenlaescuela.com)

7. **Vicente Fidel López (1815–1903):** Present from the beginning as one of the founding circle members. Son of Vicente López y Planes. Documented as a regular attendee alongside Alberdi, Gutiérrez, and Echeverría. Became an important historian of Argentine independence. His presence bridges the generation of the Himno Nacional (his father) and the new intellectual generation.

8. **The Asociación de Mayo (1838):** After the Salón closed under Rosas pressure in early 1838, Echeverría organized a clandestine successor: *Asociación de la Joven Generación Argentina* (modeled on Mazzini's Young Europe), July–August 1838. Founded by Echeverría, Alberdi, and Gutiérrez. The *Código o declaración de principios* drafted by Echeverría was the intellectual core. This became the *Dogma Socialista* (1846 edition).

9. **Certeza note on "casa de Miguel Cané":** Multiple sources mention the circle meeting first "en la casa de Miguel Cané (padre)" before the formal Salón. This is a reasonable secondary claim — not directly documented in the Weinberg primary compilation — so it should be card-hecho with a tight cite, or card-nota-certeza inline.

## Critical Facts Verified

### Echeverría
- Left Buenos Aires October 1825, arrived Paris March 1826
- Returned Buenos Aires 28 June 1830 (Aduana entry documented)
- Published *Los Consuelos* November 1834 (first Argentine romantic poetry collection)
- **Was absent from the Salón's inaugural act** — sent two cantos of *La Cautiva* instead (multiple sources agree)
- Founded *Asociación de la Joven Generación Argentina* 1838 (clandestine, modeled on Mazzini)

### Gutiérrez
- Born Buenos Aires 1809
- Law degree 1834 (Departamento de Jurisprudencia, UBA)
- Formed close friendship with Echeverría in 1834; long walks in the outskirts
- Spoke at the Salón inaugural: *Fisonomía del saber español*
- Served as **vicepresidente** of the Asociación de Mayo

### Alberdi
- Already documented extensively in the site; M015 should reference his presence briefly
- Joined the Echeverría–Gutiérrez walks "ya en 1835" (Alberdi's own memoirs)
- Gave the philosophical keynote at the Salón inaugural
- Rented a room with Gutiérrez in Mariquita Sánchez's house during this period

### Vicente López y Planes
- **Presided over the inaugural act** — confirmed by historiaybiografias.com
- His son Vicente Fidel López was a regular member of the circle
- The father of the Himno Nacional blessing the intellectual rebellion of the next generation is narratively rich

### Vicente Fidel López
- Born 1815 (youngest of the core circle)
- Regular member alongside Alberdi, Gutiérrez, Echeverría
- Collaborated in *La Moda*
- Later author of *Historia de la República Argentina* (major historiographic work)

### Marcos Sastre
- Opened his librería in 1832 (first location); moved to Calle Victoria No. 59 in May 1837
- Informal meetings began from 1832 at the librería
- Formal Salón inaugurated June 1837 with the "Ojeada filosófica" discourse
- Sastre closed under Rosas pressure in early 1838; liquidated all inventory
